Gweathlow

1.1.

  Debr a dhen a bhui.
  /dɛbrə ðɛn ə vuj/
  eats the man the food
  The man eats the food.

The first thing you may of noticed is that the words are in a different order to that of English. In these sorts of non-question sentences the word order is Verb-Subject-Object (or VSO for short), whilst English generally imploys SVO word order.

Darch is a Class I verb. They conjugate like this:

Pers. Sg. Pl.
1st debraf debran
2nd debr debr
3rd debr debran

Dhen and bhui show signs of lenition. This is a softening of the first consonant of a word before other specific kinds of words. We call this kind of consonant change mutation. You'll learn more about that later. Here's all the different types of mutation:

1.2.

  Darch y.
  he sees her
  He sees her.

1.3.

  By cy can y.
  is dog with her
  She has a dog.

1.4.

  Yw a gy mor.
  is dog big
  The dog is big.
